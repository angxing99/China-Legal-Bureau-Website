Singapore Corporation Tax

Overview of Singapore Income Tax
In Singapore, taxes are collected only by legal bodies;Under the Income Tax Act 1948 (referred to as the Act), the Inland Revenue Department of Singapore (IRAS).IRAS is the main tax collection agency of the Singapore Government.Another tax-related law in Singapore is the economic Expansion Incentives Act (EEIA) of 2005, which aims to encourage the development of targeted industries and activities.The two bills referred to are supplemented by various subsidiary legislation.This auxiliary legislation contains several comprehensive tax treaties.These treaties help prevent double taxation on cross-border transactions, promote international trade and investment by clarifying tax rules between countries, and avoid tax evasion.

 Corporate Tax rate in Singapore
Whether it is a local company or a foreign company, a company shall be taxed at a flat rate on its receivables.The corporate tax rate is 17%.

Corporate Tax exemption scheme for newly established companies in Singapore
A newly registered qualifying company may claim a 75% reduction on the first S $100,000 of normal taxable income in each of its three consecutive years of assessment (YAs) and a 50% reduction on the next S $100,000 of taxable income.

The geographical scope of Singapore's tax system
The company's source of revenue is determined by the phrase "generated or derived from Singapore".The phrase in the bill describes the territorial scope of Singapore's tax system.This means Singapore has the right to tax income if the source of income is in Singapore.The company will not be liable for income tax unless the income is accrued.Revenue cannot be attributed to the company unless the company has unfettered discretion.Another important expression in the Act is "received from outside Singapore".This leads to the taxation of income from foreign sources in Singapore, provided that the income is received in Singapore and is not exempt.However, even if the income is not remitted to Singapore, it is still taxable.Pursuant to section 10 (25) of the Act, any income derived from sources outside Singapore which is used as a set-off against any liability arising from trade or business carried on in Singapore or as a set-off against any amount arising therefrom shall be deemed taxable.

Determine the first year of assessment (YA)
• The first YA is the base period for the establishment of the company.
• New startups get a tax break, while other companies get a partial tax break starting in the fourth assessable year.Income tax base period.

Income tax base period
• YA statutory income in Singapore is based on income from all sources in the preceding calendar year (base year).This will form the basis for all tax assessments and provide guidance on annual taxable income.

Deadline for income tax returns
• Starting in 2009, Singapore companies must file their income tax by Nov. 30.Tax guidelines can be widely used by businesses if they need them.
• The company must submit a complete set of returns, including Form C, audited/unaudited accounts and tax calculations.
• All companies must file their tax returns by Nov. 30 to avoid enforcement action.The company secretary will compile and prepare the required documents.

Single income tax system  
• Singapore introduced a single-tier corporate income tax system on 1 January 2003.
• The tax paid by the Company on taxable income shall be the final tax.All dividends paid by the company to shareholders are tax-free.
• There is no capital gains tax in Singapore (including fixed asset sales, foreign exchange gains, capital transactions, etc.).
